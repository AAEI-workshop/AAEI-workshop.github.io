  • Service Robots and Large Models

Abstract

With the rising demand for intelligent services and increasing labor costs, service robots are widely used in healthcare, elderly care, education, and customer service.However, traditional service robots rely on predefined rules and limited perception, resulting in rigid interactions, poor adaptability, and insufficient environmental understanding, making them inadequate for complex scenarios.

The emergence of large models presents a transformative opportunity for service robots.Trained on massive datasets, these models excel in natural language understanding, speech interaction, image recognition, and autonomous decision-making.By integrating large models, service robots can achieve more natural human-robot interactions, precise environmental perception, and intelligent task planning, significantly enhancing their autonomy and capabilities.

Despite these advantages, integrating large models with service robots poses several challenges, including computational constraints, real-time inference optimization, model lightweighting, and data privacy concerns.To address these challenges and advance this field, we propose the "Service Robots and Large Models" Workshop.
This workshop will focus on key topics such as intelligent perception, natural interaction, autonomous decision-making, and model deployment optimization.
Bringing together experts from academia and industry, this workshop aims to foster discussions on cutting-edge research, share the latest advancements, and facilitate interdisciplinary collaboration.By bridging the gap between research and real-world applications, we seek to accelerate the development of next-generation intelligent service robots.

Content of the workshop and expected impact

This workshop aims to explore the innovative integration of service robots and large language models in real-world applications, discussing how LLM can drive breakthroughs in robot perception, decision-making, and interaction capabilities. We will focus on the following key topics:

The latest technological advancements and challenges in the field of service robots, particularly in applications for home, healthcare, and public services.
The role of LLM in enhancing the autonomy and intelligence of service robots, especially in human-robot interaction, visual perception, and behavior planning.
The co-evolution of robots and AI models, as well as the future directions driven by interdisciplinary collaboration.

This workshop will invite scholars, industry experts, and professionals to share cutting-edge results, inspire in-depth thinking about future technologies, and promote the cross-disciplinary integration and innovation of service robots and large model technologies. We will invite top scholars and corporate experts as speakers, with content closely tied to practical applications and frontier technologies, ensuring that all topics reflect the latest developments in the fields of robotics and automation. The content will explore how LLM impact various aspects of service robotics, and interactive sessions will be set up to allow participants to not only listen to expert insights but also engage in discussions on practical issues.

By combining these two rapidly evolving fields—service robots and LLM—this workshop will explore their intersection, unveiling new opportunities and technical challenges that traditional robotics cannot cover. This interdisciplinary fusion will bring a fresh perspective to IROS 2025, fostering a deep integration of robotics technology and LLM. The workshop will not only cover academic research but also emphasize industry practices, showcasing real-world examples of how LLMs are being applied in service robotics. With this diversified content structure, we aim to spark deep discussions among participants from various backgrounds, driving the collision of new ideas and technological innovations.
