About

The workshop "AI Agent and Embodied Intelligence" aims to explore the intersection of AI agents and embodied intelligence. With the rapid development of LLMs, the abilities of AI agents and embodied AI are also advancing. Although both areas focus on how intelligent systems can perceive, reason, and act in environments, their technical focuses currently differ. Therefore, this workshop will seek to identify the technical distinctions between these two fields. We aim to explore what is truly unique to UI/game/other virtual agents (tool usage, memory, screen I/O, etc.) versus embodied agents (sensor noise, reasoning in 3D environments, action generalization, safety, etc.).

We will also compare the technical pathways of AI agents and embodied AI, addressing several pressing questions: How can UI agents borrow embodied strategies for 3D spatial reasoning in desktop environments? Can game agents transfer hierarchical planning skills to robotic manipulation? What are the common benchmarks or foundation models (e.g., multimodal LLMs, diffusion policies) that can unite these fields? These discussions will challenge conventional paradigms and explore innovative designs and applications. By bringing together experts from related areas, this workshop will foster interdisciplinary dialogue to chart the future of AI agents. Join us to shape the next generation of AI agent and embodied intelligence.

Topics of Interest
Relevant topics include, but are not limited to:
  • Technical Distinctions: Comparative analysis of algorithms and architectures for virtual vs. embodied agents.
  • Cross-Domain Transfer: Techniques for transferring skills from virtual environments (games, simulators) to physical robots, and vice-versa.
  • Spatial Reasoning: Methods for 2D/3D reasoning in both desktop and physical environments.
  • Memory and Planning: Hierarchical planning, long-horizon task execution, and memory mechanisms for both agent types.
  • Foundation Models for Agents: The role of multimodal LLMs, vision-language models, and diffusion policies in unifying the fields.
  • Benchmarks and Evaluation: Novel benchmarks, tasks, or metrics for fair evaluation of both virtual and embodied agents.
  • Safety and Robustness: Handling failure modes, safety constraints, sensor noise, and unexpected inputs.
  • Human-Agent Interaction: Interaction paradigms for both virtual (UI) and embodied (robotic) agents.
