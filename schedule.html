Talk 1 Jianhao Jiao
SLAM for Robotics: Lifelong Spatial Memory and Navigation in Unstructured Worlds

Modern robotics demands that autonomous systems, from agile quadrupeds to humanoids, operate reliably in dynamic field environments to assist people in complex tasks like logistics and inspection. This utility fundamentally relies on the robot possessing a reliable, lifelong spatial memory. Traditional Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) systems use dense, high-fidelity geometric maps that are non-scalable, storage-intensive, and inherently fragile when confronted with real-world dynamism and temporal change. To overcome this crisis of scalability, this talk presents a transition towards a sparse, cognitive spatial memory architecture. We introduce OpenNavMap, a structure-free topometric mapping approach that abandons explicit 3D geometry in favor of a lightweight, multi-layered graph representation. This system achieves metric-level consistency, essential for planning, by dynamically reconstructing local 3D structure on-demand via neural inference, enabling robust cross-session map merging. This novel paradigm has been validated across diverse real-world scenarios, including deployment on mobile robotic platforms. The discussion will also conclude by focusing on the critical future challenge: enabling this spatial memory mechanism to achieve lifelong adaptability and real-time maintenance in highly unstructured and visually challenging domains, specifically addressing navigation in forests.

Talk 2 Meng Fang
Agentic Language Techniques for Decision-Making

Recent advances in large language models (LLMs) have opened a new frontier in agent-based natural language processing (NLP) — where language models are not only tools for understanding or generation, but autonomous decision-making agents capable of perceiving, reasoning, and acting in open-ended environments. This talk explores how language models can be transformed into decision-making agents through the integration of reasoning, planning, and interaction. It highlights emerging research directions that bridge NLP, reinforcement learning, and multi-agent systems toward building more adaptive, explainable, and goal-driven AI systems.

Talk 3 Ze ji
Physically-aware Learning for Robotic Manipulation of Deformable Materials

This talk explores the integration of robotics and robot learning for autonomous robot manipulation and navigation. A key focus of the talk is the challenges of robot reinforcement learning in real-world deployment. Details are provided on using reinforcement learning for manipulation and navigation challenges. This approach enables robots to acquire complex skills, navigate intricate environments, and perform multifaceted manipulation tasks effectively, such as using long-horizon manipulation, handling entangled objects, manipulating deformable materials, and tackling autonomous navigation in mapless environments. The discussion then shifts to our recently focused work on robotic manipulation of deformable materials using differentiable physics, offering a novel solution for safe and efficient learning. Through active manipulation of contact-rich tasks, our work allows robots to infer unobservable aspects of the physical world, such as the physics parameters of deformable materials, like Young's modulus and friction. This talk aims to spark discussion about progress in merging robot learning with differentiable physics models to endow robots with the ability to understand the physical world. Such advancements hold promise for a future where robots seamlessly integrate into diverse fields, achieving complex objectives.
